Frequently asked questions

Governments subsidize solar PV (photovoltaic) to accelerate the green transition, reduce pressure on energy infrastructure, and support equitable access to clean energy.

The Single-family Affordable Solar Homes program (SASH 1.0 & 2.0) provides solar incentives for owner-occupied, low-income households.

The Multi-family Affordable Solar Housing program (MASH 1.0 & 2.0) provides incentives for solar installations on qualifying multi-family affordable housing properties.

The Disadvantaged Communities - Single-family Affordable Solar Homes program is the successor to SASH, specifically targeting homeowners in the top 25% most disadvantaged communities in California.

Eligibility is defined by properties qualifying as affordable housing or households meeting annual income limits set by the California Alternate Rates for Energy (CARE) or Family Electric Rate Assistance (FERA) guidelines.

PG&E, SCE, and SDG&E are the three primary California Investor Owned Utilities (IOUs) that manage the Pacific Gas & Electric, Southern California Edison, and San Diego Gas & Electric service territories.
